Rocket fleet

Since 2008 SpaceX have launched almost 150 missions to space. After a few years of trial and error the Falcon 1 finally launched to low earth orbit (as the first privately owned rocket in history to do so). This success earned the company a billion dollar contract with NASA, and facilitated a boost in development on the Falcon 9, SpaceX's trusty workhorse.The next years development focused on the landing and rescue capabilities of the Falcon 9 boosters, a feat that has made the commercialization of space more viable.

In 2018 Falcon Heavy had its first test flight, becoming the most powerful operational rocket in the world. Currently testing of the SpaceX's Starship spacecraft and Super Heavy rocket (also called Starship) is underway. When operational it will not only de-throne Falcon Heavy as the strongest operational lifter, but become the most powerful lifter ever deployed. Elon Musk has stated that SpaceX's goal is to put man on Mars by 2026.
